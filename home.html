Le Corbusier is a Swiss-born French architect who belongs to the first generation of the International school of architecture. Le Corbusier was born Charles-Edouard Jeanneret-Gris in Switzerland on October 6, 1887. In 1917, he moved to Paris and assumed the pseudonym Le Corbusier. In his architecture, Le Corbusier mainly builds with steel and reinforced concrete and works with elemental geometric forms. Le Corbusier's painting emphasizes clear forms and structures, which corresponds to his architecture.
